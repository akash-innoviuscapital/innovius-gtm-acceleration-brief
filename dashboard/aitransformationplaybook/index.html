Day 0 — Where Are We

Every employee answers behavioral questions — not "how comfortable are you?" but "do you do this today?"

  • Have you used Claude in the last 7 days?
  • Have you set up a Project with company context?
  • Have you connected Claude to a live tool (Slack, email, Drive)?
  • Have you built a Skill that replaces a weekly task?
  • Has anyone else used something you built?
2

Day 30 — What Moved

Same instrument, 30 days later. The delta heatmap is your readout to the CEO — concrete evidence, not anecdote.

  • Individual level movement per employee
  • Company-wide level distribution shift
  • Bottom quartile: who's stuck and why?
  • Top performers flagged for champion network
  • Presented jointly: Innovius + Executive Champion
Framework · Reference Standard

The 0–10 Mastery Ladder

Practitioner floor is Level 4. Power track targets Level 6. Levels 7–10 are infrastructure roles — not the target for this program.

0
Tourist
Typing prompts with no setup. Treating Claude like ChatGPT in a browser.
1
Configured
Project created. Managing context intentionally.
2
Context Engineered
Project instructions in place. Claude knows your role before you type a word.
3
Connected
Live connectors active. Slack, Drive, email, CRM pulling real data into context.
4
Skilled Practitioner Floor
Custom Skills built. Repeatable workflows triggered by one command.
5
Scripted Power Track
Headless mode. Scripts calling Claude programmatically. JSON piping.
6
Chained Power Track
Multi-phase Skills. Subagents. One trigger runs a full workflow across multiple Claude calls.
7
Parallel
Orchestrator + specialist agents running simultaneously. You're coordinating agents, not prompting them.
8
Autonomous
Cron jobs. Event-driven triggers. Background agents running without human initiation. Claude reacts to the world.
9
Infrastructure
Claude Code embedded in production systems. Monitoring, error handling, self-healing loops. It's a service, not a tool.
10
Swarm Architect
Agents that spawn, configure, and improve other agents. Autonomous loops that adapt. Maybe 50 people on the planet.
Phase 1 · Days 1–5

Setup

Remove all friction before the humans show up. Everyone opens Claude on Day 6 and it already knows their company, their tools, and their role.

Licensing
Anthropic Max or Pro for Everyone

Teams or Enterprise license provisioned org-wide. Every seat active before Day 6. No exceptions.

Connectors
Pre-Built at the Org Level

Slack, Drive, email, CRM — whatever the company runs on. Configured centrally so no individual sets up their own integrations.

Context Layer
Company Projects Pre-Loaded

Each department gets a pre-configured Project with company context and role-specific instructions already baked in.

Critical: Infrastructure Owner

Designate an IT/Ops maintainer on Day 1

Owns license management, connector maintenance, and new hire onboarding. Separate from the Executive Champion.

Phase 2 · Days 6–15

Jumpstart

Move everyone off Level 0. Get the power users to Level 4. In-person preferred, half-day. Two tracks run simultaneously — every employee attends.

Practitioner Track

Everyone · Target 0→4
Kill the ChatGPT mental model. What Claude actually is and why it changes how you work.
Projects with real context. Set up your actual role during the session, not a hypothetical.
Connectors in daily work. Search your real email, summarize a real Slack thread, live in the room.
Build your first Skill. One weekly task, productized before you leave.
Exit bar: Every practitioner has one live Skill triggered before the session ends.

Power Track

Tech & Ops · Target 4→6
Multi-phase Skills and chained workflows. Sequential logic, one trigger across multiple steps.
Headless scripting basics. Claude calling without a browser, JSON piping, automated triggers.
Build for others, not yourself. The production-ready standard from day one.
Phase 3 selection. Your 2–3 evangelism co-builders come from this group.
Exit bar: Each participant has a chained workflow outlined and one section built.
Phase 3 · Days 16–25

Evangelize

Prove what's possible through internal voices, not an outside consultant. Innovius advises. They build. Their name on it.

The Co-Build Dynamic

Advisor and Builder — Not the Other Way Around

When they demo on Day 30, it's their demo — not ours. That's what changes culture.

Project Selection

  • Solves a problem the whole company feels
  • Multiple people can use it immediately
  • Demoed in under 5 minutes
  • Production-ready: a system, not a one-off

The Production-Ready Standard

Every project in Phase 3 — and every build in Phase 4 — must meet this bar. "Production-ready" means shareable and repeatable, not just functional.

More than one person can use it without your help
It's a repeatable system — not a one-time prompt
The trigger is documented — anyone can run it
It replaces or meaningfully accelerates a real workflow
Output is consistent enough to be trusted by a team
Can be handed off if the builder leaves

Structure · Most Critical Appointment

The Executive Champion

Not the most technical person — the most organizationally credible one. Their buy-in is a prerequisite, not an outcome.

This Is Not a Training Coordinator

The Executive Champion is an organizational authority. They set the standard, enforce the cadence, and hold the culture accountable to moving at AI-native speed.

Briefed separately before the company kickoff. Co-presents the Day 30 readout. Reports to the CEO on transformation progress — not to IT or HR.

Selection Criteria

  • Has authority to set and enforce a standard — COO, Chief of Staff, or strong VP
  • Genuinely bought in before Day 1 — not voluntold
  • Owns weekly cadence and monthly showcase after Innovius exits
  • Accountable to CEO for culture shifting, not training completion
  • Has standing to make AI fluency visible in performance conversations
  • Credible to the team — people will build for this person's showcase
